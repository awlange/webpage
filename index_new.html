excited electrons in DNA

As part of my dissertation, I studied excited electrons in DNA using quantum chemistry simulations. The red-blue blob here is a depiction of how the three dimensional wave (or, orbital) of an electron spreads out between two adenine molecules when it is excited by ultraviolet light. The red and blue indicates the phase of the electron wave. The adenine dimer is somewhat hidden by the orbital, but the dimer is in the stacking geometry found in biological DNA and surrounded by water, serving as a model to understand electronic excitation in solvated DNA.
